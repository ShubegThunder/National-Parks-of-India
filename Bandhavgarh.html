🐯 Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh, nestled in Madhya Pradesh, is a paradise for wildlife lovers. Famous for its dense tiger population and ancient ruins, it blends natural beauty with historical intrigue.

📍 Location & Terrain

Spread over 1,500 sq km including buffer zones, Bandhavgarh features tropical forests, rocky hills, meadows, and marshes. The legendary Bandhavgarh Fort adds a mystical touch to the wild setting.

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

  • October to June: Open season for safaris.
  • Nov–Feb: Pleasant, ideal for birdwatching.
  • Mar–May: High tiger sighting chances.
  • June–Sep: Park remains closed due to monsoon.

🚗 Getting There

  • By Train: Umaria (35 km), Katni (100 km)
  • By Air: Jabalpur Airport (170 km)
  • By Road: Good access via NH43

🦁 Safari Zones

  • Tala Zone: Most popular, rich tiger sightings
  • Magadhi & Khitauli: Scenic and less crowded
  • Panpatha: Buffer zone option

🎟️ Safari Details

  • Jeep Safari: ₹5,000 – ₹6,000 per vehicle
  • Canter Safari: ₹1,500 – ₹2,000 per person
  • Entry Fees: ₹250 (Indian), ₹950 (Foreigner)
  • Book online; ID required

🛏️ Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Mahua Kothi, Kings Lodge
  • Mid-range: Mogli Jungle Resort
  • Budget: Forest Rest Houses in Tala

📸 Top Things to Do

  • Morning & evening safaris
  • Visit Bandhavgarh Fort and Shesh Shaiya
  • Birdwatching at Gopalpur pond
  • Nature trails with local guides

🧭 Conservation Tips

  • Stick to designated trails
  • Carry out your trash
  • Support eco-tourism efforts
  • Don't disturb wildlife or habitats
