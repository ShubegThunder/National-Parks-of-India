🐅 Bandipur National Park

Bandipur National Park, located in Karnataka, is one of India’s premier tiger reserves and a key part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. Known for its dry deciduous forests and grassy woodlands, it’s a haven for tigers, elephants, and countless other species.

📍 Location & Landscape

Situated in the Chamarajanagar district of Karnataka, Bandipur spans over 874 sq km. It shares boundaries with Mudumalai Wildlife Sanctuary in Tamil Nadu, Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary in Kerala, and Nagarhole National Park in Karnataka, creating a vast contiguous wildlife habitat.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: Best for wildlife spotting and safaris
  • June to September: Monsoon season—lush and green, but some safari restrictions may apply

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Mysore Airport (80 km), Bengaluru International Airport (220 km)
  • By Road: Well connected via NH766 from Mysore, Bangalore, and Ooty
  • By Train: Nearest station is Mysore (around 80 km)

🦁 Wildlife & Activities

  • Jeep and bus safaris to spot tigers, elephants, leopards, gaurs, and wild dogs
  • Birdwatching—over 200 recorded bird species
  • Nature walks and photography tours (in designated zones)

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry + safari: approx. ₹350–₹650 per person (varies by season and vehicle)
  • No littering, no noise, and strictly no feeding wildlife
