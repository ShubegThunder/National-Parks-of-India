🦋 Bannerghatta National Park

Located just outside Bengaluru, Karnataka, Bannerghatta National Park is a unique blend of a natural reserve, a zoo, a butterfly park, and a safari experience — offering visitors a close encounter with India’s rich wildlife and biodiversity.

📍 Location & Landscape

Spread over about 260 square kilometers, Bannerghatta encompasses dry deciduous forests, scrubland, and hilly terrain. It is home to elephants, leopards, bears, and numerous bird species, along with a conservation-focused zoo and rescue center.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Pleasant weather, ideal for safaris and outdoor exploration
  • April to June: Hot, but good for spotting animals near waterholes
  • July to September: Lush and green during monsoon, but with occasional rain interruptions

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru (70 km)
  • By Road: 22 km from Bengaluru city center via NH 48
  • By Train: Bengaluru City Railway Station (23 km)

🦁 Wildlife & Activities

  • Wildlife safaris to spot lions, tigers, bears, and elephants
  • Butterfly Park featuring over 20 species of butterflies
  • Trekking trails in adjoining forest areas
  • Zoo and Rescue Center for wildlife rehabilitation

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry fee: approx. ₹80 (adults), safari charges extra (₹260–₹500 depending on type)
  • Photography charges may apply for cameras
  • No littering, feeding animals, or making loud noises — respect the natural environment
