🦁 Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park, located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, is India’s oldest national park and a cornerstone of Indian wildlife conservation. Established in 1936 and named after the legendary hunter and naturalist Jim Corbett, the park is famous for its significant population of Bengal tigers and rich biodiversity, nestled against the backdrop of the lower Himalayas.

📍 Location and Geography

The park spans over 520 square kilometers and includes hills, riverine belts, grasslands, and a large lake. The Ramganga River winds through the park, supporting diverse ecosystems. The altitude ranges from 400m to 1,200m above sea level, creating varied habitats.

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

  • November to mid-June is the ideal time to visit.
  • The park is closed during monsoon (July to mid-November).
  • Winter (Nov–Feb): Pleasant weather and great for photography.
  • Summer (Mar–Jun): Hot but improves chances of tiger sightings.

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Train: Ramnagar Station (12 km from the park gate)
  • By Road: 250 km from Delhi (approx. 5–6 hours)
  • By Air: Pantnagar Airport (80 km), Delhi Airport (major option)

🏕️ Zones of the Park

  • Dhikala: Most popular zone, rich in wildlife. Allows overnight stays.
  • Bijrani: Grasslands and forested areas. Good for tiger sightings.
  • Jhirna: Open all year. Great for birdwatchers.
  • Dhela: Less crowded and scenic.
  • Durga Devi: Hilly terrain, ideal for birding.
  • Sitabani: Buffer zone with walking trails.

🎟️ Safari & Entry Fees (Approx.)

  • Jeep Safari (6 persons): ₹4,500 – ₹6,000
  • Canter Safari (Dhikala): ₹1,500 – ₹2,000 per person
  • Entry Fee: ₹200–₹300 (Indians), ₹1,000–₹1,500 (Foreigners)
  • Note: Advance booking and valid ID proof are mandatory.

📸 Popular Activities

  • Morning and evening Jeep Safaris
  • Canter Safari in Dhikala zone
  • Birdwatching with over 600 species
  • Photography at sunrise and sunset
  • Nature Walks in Sitabani zone
  • Seasonal River Rafting near Garjiya and Mohan

🎒 What to Pack

  • Earth-toned, comfortable clothes
  • Sturdy walking shoes
  • Binoculars, camera, flashlight
  • Sunhat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Reusable water bottle
  • ID proof and printed safari permit

🏨 Accommodation

  • Forest Rest Houses (FRHs): Located inside the park (e.g., Dhikala, Bijrani)
  • Eco-resorts and lodges: Range from budget to luxury, near Ramnagar
  • Government Guest Houses: Affordable, basic amenities

Book in advance — park accommodations fill up quickly, especially in peak season.

♻️ Conservation Rules

  • No plastic, loud music, or alcohol inside the park
  • Stay inside the vehicle during safaris
  • Do not feed or disturb animals
  • Follow park guide and rules at all times

💡 Travel Tips

  • Book safaris at least 15–30 days in advance
  • Early morning safaris offer better wildlife sightings
  • Stay near the park gate or inside for convenience
  • Enjoy the journey — tiger sightings are not guaranteed, but the park is a natural treasure regardless

🧭 Final Thoughts

Jim Corbett National Park offers an unforgettable experience that blends raw wilderness, scenic landscapes, and thrilling encounters with India’s most iconic wildlife. Whether you’re an avid birdwatcher, a family traveler, or a wildlife photographer, Corbett has something extraordinary to offer.
