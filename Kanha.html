🌳 Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park, located in Madhya Pradesh, is often called the inspiration behind Rudyard Kipling’s *The Jungle Book*. It’s one of India’s most well-maintained tiger reserves and a haven for the rare Barasingha (swamp deer).

📍 Location & Landscape

The park spans over 940 sq km in the Maikal hills of the Satpura range. It features lush sal and bamboo forests, grassy meadows, and meandering streams, making it one of the most picturesque reserves in India.

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

  • October to June: Official visiting season
  • March to May: Best for spotting tigers and other wildlife
  • November to February: Pleasant and scenic, ideal for photography

🚗 Getting There

  • By Air: Jabalpur Airport (160 km), Raipur Airport (250 km)
  • By Train: Gondia (145 km), Jabalpur (160 km)
  • By Road: Well connected from Nagpur, Raipur, and Jabalpur

🐅 Safari Zones

  • Kanha Zone: Core area with good tiger density
  • Kisli Zone: Offers a mix of forest and grassland
  • Sarhi & Mukki Zones: Less crowded, scenic, great for birdwatching

🎟️ Safari Fees (Approx.)

  • Jeep Safari: ₹6,500 – ₹7,000 per vehicle
  • Guide Fee: ₹360 per trip
  • Entry Fee: ₹250 (Indian), ₹1,000 (Foreigner)
  • Online booking highly recommended

🏨 Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Taj Banjaar Tola, Kanha Earth Lodge
  • Mid-range: Kipling Camp, Kanha Jungle Lodge
  • Budget: Forest guesthouses in Kisli and Mukki

📸 Must-Do Experiences

  • Jeep safari in early morning mist
  • Visit the Kanha Museum of Life & Art
  • Watch Barasinghas grazing in open meadows
  • Enjoy birdwatching and peaceful forest walks

🌱 Conservation Focus

  • Kanha is pivotal in the conservation of Barasingha
  • Strict eco-tourism practices are followed
  • Plastic-free and noise-controlled safaris
