🐅 Manas National Park

Manas National Park, located in Assam, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rare and endangered wildlife, including the golden langur, pygmy hog, and Bengal tiger.

📍 Location & Landscape

Situated at the foothills of the Himalayas along the Manas River, the park spans about 950 sq km. Its landscape features grasslands, tropical forests, and riverine ecosystems, offering a pristine biodiversity haven.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • November to April: Cool and dry, ideal for wildlife sightings
  • May to October: Monsoon affects accessibility; avoid travel during this time

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi Airport, Guwahati (approx. 180 km)
  • By Road: Connected via NH-27 from Guwahati to Barpeta Road
  • By Train: Barpeta Road Railway Station (approx. 20 km)

🦏 Wildlife & Activities

  • Jeep safaris to spot tigers, elephants, and rhinoceros
  • Birdwatching—home to over 450 species of birds
  • River rafting on the Manas River

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry + safari fees: ₹200–₹500 (varies by season and nationality)
  • Respect forest rules, maintain silence during safaris, and carry back waste
