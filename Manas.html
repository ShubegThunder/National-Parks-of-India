Manas National Park, located in Assam, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rare and endangered wildlife, including the golden langur, pygmy hog, and Bengal tiger.

Situated at the foothills of the Himalayas along the Manas River, the park spans about 950 sq km. Its landscape features grasslands, tropical forests, and riverine ecosystems, offering a pristine biodiversity haven.
