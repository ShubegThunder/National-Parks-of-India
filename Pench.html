🐾 Pench National Park

Pench National Park, straddling the borders of Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra, is famed as the setting for Rudyard Kipling’s *The Jungle Book*. It's a haven for wildlife and nature lovers with its scenic landscapes and rich biodiversity.

📍 Location & Landscape

Pench spreads over 758 sq km of forest and grasslands. The Pench River flows through the park, supporting a variety of flora and fauna including tigers, leopards, wild dogs, and over 200 bird species.

🗓️ Best Time to Visit

  • Best months: October to June
  • Summer (Mar–Jun): High chance of spotting big cats
  • Winter (Nov–Feb): Pleasant weather for exploring

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Nagpur Airport (92 km away)
  • By Rail: Seoni or Nagpur stations
  • By Road: Well-connected via NH44

🌳 Key Attractions

  • Jungle safaris (morning/evening)
  • Night safari in buffer zones
  • Trekking and birdwatching
  • Local village culture and handicrafts

🎒 Tips & Essentials

  • Wear neutral-colored clothing
  • Bring a camera with zoom lens
  • Stay hydrated and carry ID proof
  • Maintain silence during safaris
