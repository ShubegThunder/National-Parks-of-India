🐅 Rajaji National Park

Rajaji National Park, located in Uttarakhand, is a beautiful wildlife reserve known for its elephants, tigers, and the stunning Shivalik mountain ranges. It forms an important part of the Indo-Gangetic plains' ecosystem.

📍 Location & Landscape

The park spans over 820 square kilometers across Haridwar, Dehradun, and Pauri Garhwal districts. It features a mix of sal forests, riverine vegetation, grasslands, and rocky terrain along the foothills of the Himalayas.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • November to June: Ideal for safaris and wildlife spotting
  • July to October: Monsoon season — park remains closed for visitors

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (35 km)
  • By Road: Well connected to Haridwar, Rishikesh, and Dehradun by highways
  • By Train: Haridwar Railway Station (20 km) and Dehradun Railway Station (56 km)

🦁 Wildlife & Activities

  • Jeep safaris to spot elephants, tigers, leopards, and goral
  • Birdwatching — over 300 species of birds, including hornbills and kingfishers
  • Nature walks and photography along scenic trails
  • Rafting in nearby Rishikesh (outside park limits)

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry fee: approx. ₹150 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners), safari charges extra
  • Always maintain silence and follow the guide’s instructions
  • No plastic, loud noises, or feeding wildlife
