🌿 Silent Valley National Park

Silent Valley National Park, located in Kerala’s Nilgiri Hills, is one of India’s last undisturbed tracts of tropical evergreen forest. Known for its rich biodiversity and pristine beauty, it is a paradise for nature lovers and researchers alike.

📍 Location & Landscape

Situated in the Palakkad district of Kerala, Silent Valley covers about 236 sq km. The park’s lush forests, steep ridges, and river valleys form part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. The Kuntipuzha River flows through the park, sustaining its unique ecosystem.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • December to April: Pleasant weather for trekking and wildlife viewing
  • May to November: Monsoon season—lush but heavy rains can limit access

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Coimbatore International Airport (91 km)
  • By Road: Nearest town is Mannarkkad, well-connected to Palakkad and Coimbatore
  • By Train: Palakkad Junction (66 km)

🦋 Wildlife & Activities

  • Home to lion-tailed macaques, Nilgiri langurs, tigers, leopards, and Malabar giant squirrels
  • Over 200 bird species, including the Malabar trogon and great Indian hornbill
  • Nature trekking, guided jeep safaris, and photography
  • Botanical richness with countless endemic plant species

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry fee: approx. ₹50 for Indians, ₹150 for foreigners (extra for safaris)
  • No plastic, no loud noises, and follow eco-tourism guidelines strictly
