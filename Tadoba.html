🐅 Tadoba National Park

Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, located in Maharashtra, is one of India’s oldest and largest national parks, famous for its thriving tiger population and pristine teak forests.

📍 Location & Landscape

Situated in Chandrapur district, Maharashtra, Tadoba covers about 1,727 sq km, including the Tadoba National Park and the Andhari Wildlife Sanctuary. The landscape features a mix of dry deciduous forest, grasslands, bamboo thickets, and the scenic Tadoba Lake.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • October to June: Best for tiger sightings and safaris, especially in summer months
  • July to September: Park remains closed during monsoon for most tourism zones

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport, Nagpur (140 km)
  • By Road: Well connected to Nagpur, Chandrapur, and other nearby towns
  • By Train: Chandrapur Railway Station (45 km) or Nagpur Railway Station (140 km)

🦁 Wildlife & Activities

  • Jeep safaris for tiger, leopard, sloth bear, and wild dog sightings
  • Over 195 species of birds, including crested serpent eagle and paradise flycatcher
  • Photography, guided nature walks, and visits to Tadoba Lake for crocodile spotting

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Safari costs range from ₹1,000–₹6,000 per vehicle, depending on zone and type
  • No loud noises, no littering, and follow all forest department regulations
