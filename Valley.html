🌸 Valley of Flowers National Park

Valley of Flowers National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Uttarakhand, is a breathtaking alpine meadow famous for its seasonal bloom of hundreds of species of wildflowers and its stunning Himalayan backdrop.

📍 Location & Landscape

Located in Chamoli district, Uttarakhand, the park covers about 87 sq km in the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve. The valley sits at an altitude of around 3,500–6,500 meters and is surrounded by snow-clad peaks, glacial streams, and lush meadows.

⚠️ Best Time to Visit

  • July to September: Peak bloom season when the valley bursts into vibrant colors
  • October to June: Inaccessible due to snow and harsh winter conditions

🚗 How to Reach

  • By Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (295 km)
  • By Road: Drive to Govindghat, then trek 16 km via Ghangaria to reach the valley
  • By Train: Rishikesh Railway Station (around 276 km), Haridwar (300 km)

🦋 Flora, Fauna & Activities

  • Over 500 species of flowering plants, including blue poppies, orchids, and Himalayan bellflowers
  • Wildlife sightings such as Himalayan tahr, snow leopard (rare), musk deer, and various butterflies
  • Trekking, photography, and nature walks through marked trails

🎟️ Fees & Etiquette

  • Entry fee: approx. ₹150 for Indians, ₹600 for foreigners (valid for 3 days)
  • No camping or overnight stays inside the valley; carry back all waste and respect fragile habitats
